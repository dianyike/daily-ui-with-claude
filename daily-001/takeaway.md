# Takeaway — Daily 001 Sign Up

7-field mechanism card. Per `takeaway-loop.md` self-review: each line should still apply with different brand / palette / copy / industry.

```yaml
archetype: |
  Atmospheric Photo-Led (page) + Editorial Manuscript (form as a chapter section).
  Hierarchical combination — media panel is parent, form is a body section, NOT two equal regions.

purpose_triad: |
  be-persuaded × low density.
  Success criterion observable: 3s emotional read + 10s first input typed.
  Pattern fits any single-action conversion screen (sign-up, newsletter join, contact open).

palette_recipe: |
  Committed two-tone — deep neutral (fg-token) and cream (bg-token) bisecting the canvas vertically.
  Single saturated accent reserved exclusively for primary CTA fill.
  Zero gradient, zero glow, zero third color. Anti-default: any palette layer that adds an "atmospheric" tint
  to either panel collapses into SaaS-marketing gravity within one squint.

type_pair: |
  Role split, not family list.
  Latin-sans-chrome (metadata strip / form labels / button text) — neutral, hugs structure.
  CJK-protagonist-serif (title + media caption) — carries gravitas; appears only on the two highest-tier
  emotional carriers, never on chrome.
  If publisher tokens lock the chrome family (e.g., Tech preset bound to Inter), apply the protagonist
  family via direct fontName at compose — accept non-propagation trade-off rather than dilute the
  serif appearance. Parallel to the leading resolve-then-set pattern.

motifs: |
  1. Chapter prefix echoed at two scales — same numeral, two reading positions (e.g., small eyebrow
     above the form + small plate-label inside the media); reader perceives a magazine spread, not a UI.
  2. Short hairline rules (~50-60px wide) as paragraph-break punctuation, never as full-width
     section dividers. Functions like a margin tab in a notebook — anti-SaaS-divider.
  3. Editorial caption typeset like a paperback opening line — full sentence with terminal punctuation,
     hung at media-panel bottom. Frames the conversion as continuation of a literary moment,
     not a discrete CTA prompt.

why_it_works: |
  The form panel is treated as the *second chapter* of the cinematic left panel — not a separate UI
  region. The chapter prefix on the form ("01 ／ ...") mirrors the metadata strip rhetoric on the
  media panel ("DAILY UI ／ 001"), framing form-completion as narrative continuation rather than
  conversion-event. This single rhetorical move is what lifts the layout out of "split-screen sign-up"
  SaaS-template gravity.

figma_url: https://www.figma.com/design/bDA9MWzNRM9My6grhlE8fj/daily-UI-100?node-id=41-15
```
